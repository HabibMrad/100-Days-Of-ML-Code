# History of Artificial Intelligence (1954–Today)

---

## Slide 1 — 1954–1956: Foundations and Birth of AI
- **1954:** Early machine translation and symbolic processing experiments gain momentum.
- **1955:** John McCarthy coins the term **"Artificial Intelligence"** in the Dartmouth proposal.
- **1956:** Dartmouth Summer Research Project formally launches AI as a field.
- Core belief: "Every aspect of learning or intelligence can in principle be described so precisely that a machine can simulate it."

---

## Slide 2 — 1957–1969: Optimism and Early Symbolic Systems
- **1957:** Frank Rosenblatt introduces the **Perceptron** (early neural model).
- **1958:** John McCarthy creates **LISP**, a foundational AI programming language.
- Systems solve algebra, prove theorems, and play games in constrained environments.
- Funding grows rapidly due to strong early promises.

---

## Slide 3 — 1970s: First AI Winter
- Early systems fail to scale to real-world complexity.
- Limited compute power and data hold back progress.
- Critical reports (e.g., UK Lighthill Report, 1973) reduce funding.
- AI enters its first major slowdown, called the **AI Winter**.

---

## Slide 4 — 1980s: Expert Systems Boom
- Rule-based **expert systems** (e.g., XCON) see commercial success.
- Enterprises adopt AI for diagnostics, configuration, and decision support.
- Japan’s Fifth Generation project drives global competition.
- High costs and brittle rules eventually trigger another decline.

---

## Slide 5 — Late 1980s to 1990s: Statistical ML Rises
- Shift from hand-coded rules to **data-driven learning**.
- Probabilistic models, decision trees, SVMs, and ensembles gain traction.
- **1986:** Backpropagation popularized for training multi-layer neural nets.
- **1997:** IBM Deep Blue defeats Garry Kasparov in chess.

---

## Slide 6 — 2000–2011: Data + Compute Era
- Internet growth provides massive datasets.
- GPUs and distributed computing improve training speed.
- AI strengthens in speech recognition, recommendation, and search ranking.
- **2009+:** ImageNet dataset helps standardize large-scale visual learning.

---

## Slide 7 — 2012–2016: Deep Learning Breakthroughs
- **2012:** AlexNet dramatically improves ImageNet performance.
- Deep neural networks become dominant in vision and speech.
- **2014:** GANs introduced for realistic data generation.
- **2016:** AlphaGo defeats Lee Sedol, a landmark in reinforcement learning.

---

## Slide 8 — 2017–2020: Transformer Revolution
- **2017:** "Attention Is All You Need" introduces the **Transformer** architecture.
- Pretraining + fine-tuning paradigm scales across NLP tasks.
- Models like BERT, GPT, and T5 push state-of-the-art language understanding.
- Transfer learning and foundation models begin reshaping AI development.

---

## Slide 9 — 2021–2023: Generative AI Goes Mainstream
- Large language models become widely accessible via APIs and chat products.
- Text-to-image and multimodal systems (e.g., diffusion models) accelerate creativity tools.
- Enterprises rapidly integrate copilots, assistants, and automation workflows.
- Public focus shifts to AI safety, bias, misinformation, and regulation.

---

## Slide 10 — 2024–Today: Agentic, Multimodal, and Responsible AI
- Frontier models process text, image, audio, video, and code in unified systems.
- Agentic workflows expand: planning, tool-use, retrieval, and automation.
- Efficiency advances (quantization, distillation, optimized inference) broaden deployment.
- Key priorities: alignment, transparency, governance, and human-AI collaboration.

---

## Suggested Closing Message
**AI history shows alternating cycles of hype, setbacks, and breakthroughs—driven by ideas, data, and compute. The next era will depend as much on responsible deployment as on technical progress.**
