## Slide 2 — 1954–1960: Foundations and the Birth of AI
- **1954**: Georgetown-IBM experiment demonstrates machine translation of Russian to English (rule-based)
- **1956**: Dartmouth Workshop (McCarthy, Minsky, Shannon, Rochester) coins the term **“Artificial Intelligence”**
- Early optimism: belief that human-level intelligence might be solved in a few decades
- Dominant approach: **symbolic reasoning** and handcrafted rules

---

## Slide 3 — 1960s: Early Programs and Big Promises
- Notable systems:
  - **Logic Theorist** and **General Problem Solver** (Newell & Simon)
  - **ELIZA** (Weizenbaum, 1966), early conversational program
  - **Perceptron** work (Rosenblatt) sparks interest in neural networks
- Government funding increases, especially in the U.S.
- Limitations appear in language understanding, vision, and common sense

---

## Slide 4 — 1970s: First AI Winter
- Expectations outpace results; systems fail in real-world complexity
- **1973 Lighthill Report** criticizes progress in the UK, reducing funding
- Hardware and data limitations constrain model performance
- Period known as the **first AI winter** (reduced investment and hype)

---

## Slide 5 — 1980s: Expert Systems Boom (and Bust)
- AI rebounds via **expert systems** (e.g., XCON at DEC)
- Companies encode domain knowledge into rule-based engines
- Commercial success in diagnostics, configuration, and decision support
- Challenges:
  - Knowledge engineering is costly and brittle
  - Systems struggle with uncertainty and change
- Late 1980s leads to a **second AI winter**

---

## Slide 6 — 1990s: Statistical Machine Learning Era
- Shift from hand-written rules to **data-driven statistical methods**
- Key methods rise: decision trees, SVMs, Bayesian networks, ensemble learning
- **1997**: IBM **Deep Blue** defeats chess champion Garry Kasparov
- Internet growth increases available data for learning algorithms

---

## Slide 7 — 2000s: Data, Compute, and Practical AI
- Web-scale data and cheaper compute transform applied ML
- AI widely used in search ranking, spam filtering, ads, and recommendations
- Advances in speech recognition and computer vision continue
- GPUs begin to accelerate neural network training

---

## Slide 8 — 2010s: Deep Learning Breakthroughs
- **2012**: AlexNet wins ImageNet by a large margin, catalyzing deep learning adoption
- Key architectures and advances:
  - CNNs for vision, RNN/LSTM for sequence tasks
  - **2014–2016**: GANs, seq2seq, attention mechanisms
- **2016**: DeepMind’s **AlphaGo** defeats Lee Sedol
- Industry-scale AI becomes central to cloud platforms and products

---

## Slide 9 — 2020s: Foundation Models and Generative AI
- Transformer-based foundation models dominate NLP and multimodal AI
- Milestones:
  - GPT-3, ChatGPT, GPT-4 class models
  - Diffusion models for image generation
  - Rapid progress in coding assistants and copilots
- Emerging trends: multimodal agents, tool use, retrieval augmentation, on-device models

---

## Slide 10 — Today and What’s Next
- AI is now a general-purpose technology across healthcare, science, education, finance, and manufacturing
- Core opportunities:
  - Productivity and creativity augmentation
  - Scientific discovery acceleration
  - Personalized services at scale
- Core challenges:
  - Safety, bias, misinformation, privacy, energy use, labor impact
- Future outlook: more capable, interactive, and trustworthy AI systems with stronger governance
